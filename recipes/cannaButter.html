CannaButter (Cannabic Butter)

Hello and welcome brother! We're going to be making cannabutter, and yes, it's psychoactive.

IMPORTANT!: Edibles are way stronger than smoking, the trip also last longer and takes up to 2 hours to hit. So, make sure you're in a nice area, you don't have anything important to do and always be patience.

Ingredients:

  • 100 grams of unsalted butter.
  • 1/2 cup of whole milk.
  • 2 tbsp of sugar.
  • 1 cup of water.
  • 5 to 10 grams of cannabis.

Decarboxylate the Cannabis

Before we can start making the butter, we first need to decarb our herbs. Without this process, your cannabutter won't hit. So this process is crucial to ensure a nice trip.

In order to decarb our herbs, we need to carefully cook them for at least an hour in a very controlled setting, fucking up our herbs is easy as well as not decarbing at all. So pay attention.

Ideally you want to roast your produce in the oven at 110°C to 120°C (230°F to 250°F) for at least 40 minutes. So start by pre-heating your oven at that temperature for half an hour.

While your oven pre-heats, start reducing your buds to little pieces, use your hands, you don't want to use a cutter.

Cover a baking sheet with aluminium foil or parchment paper, place the cannabis bits on top and put them in the oven.

Half-way through the process, take out the sheet from the oven, flip the bits upside down and put them again for the next half.

After baking for 40 minutes, you can now take your herbs and leave them to rest at room temperature while we start working on the butter.

Making the Butter

Start by grinding your cannabis bits, as fine as you can go without loosing too much, grind them over a paper so you don't loose any keef.

Place a small pot on low heat, add the water, once the water is boiling, add the butter and lower the flame (or voltage) as low as you can go, the idea is to gently simmer our butter, we don't want it to boil so don't go over 90°C (200°F).

Once the butter is all melted, add you fine herbs. Leave the pot uncover for at least 2 Hours, the more you simmer, the better will be. Stir once every 20 minutes or so. Remember, Do not boil the butter!

Once the 2 hours have passed, you can now filter your butter using a cheesecloth or coffee filter, I usually grind everything so fine that I don't care about bits, any chlorophyll (which could make you tummy hurt) is all oxidized by now. So don't worry. I've never had any issues but if you're princess or you're cooking for a lady, please filter your butter.

Once filter (or not) just leave your butter to rest, you can place it in the refrigerator. And Voila! Your butter is ready! So go get fucked!
